Yellow leaves are one of the most common plant complaints, and they can come from several different causes — let's narrow it down.

**Step 1: Where on the plant is the yellowing?**
- **Older/lower leaves only** → often normal aging, or nitrogen deficiency, or the plant naturally shedding leaves it no longer needs
- **New growth/upper leaves** → often points to iron, manganese, or other micronutrient deficiency, or a pH problem blocking nutrient uptake
- **Whole plant, all at once** → usually overwatering, root rot, or a sudden environmental shock

**Step 2: What does the yellowing pattern look like?**
- **Solid yellow, leaf drops soon after** → classic overwatering sign
- **Yellow with green veins still visible** → nutrient deficiency (often iron or magnesium)
- **Yellow with brown crispy edges/tips** → underwatering, low humidity, or salt/fertilizer buildup
- **Yellow spots or blotches, maybe with dark centers** → possible fungal or bacterial disease

**Step 3: Check the roots and soil**
- Gently pull the plant from its pot (or check if the soil stays soggy for days) — mushy, dark, or smelly roots = root rot from overwatering
- Dry, pulling-away-from-the-pot soil = underwatering
- White crust on soil surface = mineral/fertilizer buildup

**Step 4: Think about recent changes**
- New pot, new location, recent repotting, temperature swings, or a change in watering schedule can all trigger yellowing as a stress response
